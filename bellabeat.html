Bellabeat Case Study 🏃

A Capstone Project from the Google Data Analytics Certification

The second case study in the last course of the Google Data Analytics program provides an opportunity for students to practice the knowledge and skills learned in the program by creating a data-driven marketing strategy for Bellabeat, a high-tech manufacturer of health-focused products for women.

For this case study, I focused on gaining insights on the profile and habits of users of smart health devices that can influence how they would use one of Bellabeat’s products – the Spring bottle.

This data analysis is guided by the six steps of the data analysis process: ask, prepare, process, analyze, share, and act.

Scenario

In this case study, I am a junior data analyst working on the marketing analyst team at Bellabeat, a high-tech manufacturer of health-focused products for women. Based on the given scenario, I identified my three main tasks for this data analysis:

  1. Focus on one of Bellabeat’s products and analyze smart device data to gain insight into how consumers are using their smart devices.
  2. Gain insights to help guide marketing strategy for the company.
  3. Present high-level recommendations for Bellabeat’s marketing strategy.

Product

The Bellabeat products specified in the given data analysis scenario are the Bellabeat app, the Leaf wellness tracker, the Time wellness watch, the Spring smart water bottle, and the Bellabeat membership.

I was tasked to focus on one Bellabeat product. For this analysis, I am interested on the Bellabeat Spring water bottle. My goal is to gain insights on the health profile of users to learn important information on physical activity and water intake.

Ask

Business task

As a junior data analyst, I am expected to analyze data on the use of smart health and fitness devices to provide data-driven insights on how Bellabeat can improve its marketing strategy.

Key stakeholders

The following are the key stakeholders considered in the case study:

  1. Urška Sršen: Bellabeat’s cofounder and Chief Creative Officer
  2. Sando Mur: Mathematician and Bellabeat’s cofounder; key member of the Bellabeat executive team
  3. Bellabeat marketing analytics team: A team of data analysts responsible for collecting, analyzing, and reporting data that helps guide Bellabeat’s marketing strategy.

Prepare

Data source

The FitBit Fitness Tracker Data from Kaggle was recommended by the stakeholders. Despite some limitations, I decided to focus the data analysis to this dataset for now. The dataset is licensed under the CC0: Public Domain.

Data credibility

The dataset is generated by respondents to a distributed survey via Amazon Mechanical Turk between 03.12.2016 - 05.12.2016. The survey included 30 respondents, which is the minimum number of samples to glean reliable findings. However, there is limited information based on important demographics that is relevant to the analysis. Additional data such as age and gender can improve the analysis since Bellabeat’s products are focused on women.

Data organization

This Kaggle data set contains personal fitness tracker from thirty FitBit users. Thirty eligible Fitbit users consented to the submission of personal tracker data, including minute-level output for physical activity, heart rate, and sleep monitoring. It includes information about daily activity, steps, and heart rate that can be used to explore users’ habits.

The data set contains 18 CSV files. Some files contained merged information from other files included in the data set. Wide and narrow formats are available for some CSV files. Most files are categorized based on frequency: minute, hourly, and daily.

Data sorting and filtering

For the sake of the data analysis, only relevant data sets are going to be used. It can be observed that some data sets contain similar and redundant information, so this was also considered. The following data sets are chosen and will be sorted and filtered out further in the next stage of the data analysis:

  1. weightLogInfo_merged.csv
  2. dailyActivity_merged.csv
  3. hourlyIntensities_merged.csv

Data summary

Find number of minutes spent active vs. sedentary daily
daily_activity_mins <- daily_activity_mins %>%
mutate(total_active_minutes = very_active_minutes + fairly_active_minutes + lightly_active_minutes)
Average the number of active and sedentary minutes of each participant
activity_mins_avg <- aggregate(cbind(lightly_active_minutes,fairly_active_minutes,
                                    very_active_minutes,total_active_minutes,sedentary_minutes) ~ id,
data = daily_activity_mins, FUN = function(x) {round(mean(x))}) %>%

Find recommended average daily water intake based on average weight

Weight is an important factor in determining the average daily water intake. The following formulas were used to compute for the average water intake based on the average weight of the participants.

Without factoring in physical activity: Amount of water intake per day (oz.) = Weight (lbs.) x 0.5

Factoring in physical activity: Amount of water intake per day (oz.) = Weight (lbs.) x 0.5 + (mins. of exercise/30 mins. x 12 oz.)

Findings

The following are the insights gained based on the results of the data analysis:

  1. Most of the participants in the data set live a sedentary lifestyle. 81.61% of participants are physically inactive throughout the day.
  2. On average, participants engage in the most intense physical activities during Saturdays at 1 PM. Based on top findings, participants are most active on afternoons and early in the evening during Monday, Wednesday, Friday, and Saturday.
  3. From the average weight of the participants, around 2.5 liters of water should be consumed daily. If factoring in 30 minutes of physical activity throughout the day, almost half a liter should be added in (2.9 liters). Expressing these quantities as number of Spring bottles, around 5-6 bottles should be consumed on a daily basis.

Act

Recommendations

Based on the findings of my analysis, I listed marketing strategies and practices that Bellabeat stakeholders can consider. The recommendations listed below are mostly digital marketing strategies since the company has extensively built their online presence. In addition to this, I have also listed notes on possible product improvement and future analysis efforts.

Bellabeat marketing strategy
  1. It is a concerning fact that most of the participants in the data set live a sedentary lifestyle. The marketing team can effectively advertise the importance of their products by putting an emphasis on the findings gained from this case study. The following are specific steps they can do:
    • Launch an awareness campaign on their online platforms about the importance of living an active lifestyle. Highlight the findings from this case study and include how Bellabeat products can help by monitoring daily physical activity.
    • Using the Bellabeat app and Leaf tracker, the company can specifically target users who are sedentary and encourage them to be more active through customized reminders or advertising.
  2. The stakeholders can now consider the insights gained from the time and day users are engaging in more intense physical activities in their marketing strategies. The following can be considered:
    • Users are most likely to use their products when they are engaging in physical activity. The team can consider to put out more advertising on their online platforms or in their Bellabeat application during these times.
    • The team can put out more exercise-related notifications like hydration during times of frequent and intense physical activity.
  3. Putting the recommended average water intake into consideration, the team can implement ways to encourage users to hydrate. For example:
    • An online ad campaign that visualizes how many Spring bottles they should consume on average daily can help users understand better the quantity of water that they should be drinking.
    • The team can design customized hydration goals in their application based on the weight and physical activity profile of users. Using the tracking capabilities of the Spring bottle, the team can use positive reinforcement by giving out rewards like shop vouchers to users who are consistently meeting their hydration goals.
    • The stakeholders might consider launching larger Spring bottle sizes given the findings from the analysis. Users might consider a larger bottle size given the fact that they might refill their current Spring bottles multiple times throughout the day.
Future analysis
  1. Using company data taken from actual Bellabeat products for future analysis efforts is recommended.
  2. Insights gained from the average weight of users might be inaccurate due to a small sample size. Also, other information such as age and gender should be considered given that company’s products are targeted for women. It can be helpful to consider other data sources that provide more information about users’ profiles.
  3. Including data about water intake from Spring bottles can be useful for future analysis.
