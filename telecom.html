Telecom Customer Churn 📡

An EDA and Dashboard Project inspired by the Maven Churn Challenge

This data exploration and visualization project is inspired by a data challenge I found from Maven Analytics. The dataset provided contains churn data from 7,043 customers from a fictional telecommunications company in California in the second quarter of 2022. Information provided about the customers include their demographics, location, tenure, subscription services, status for the quarter (joined, stayed, or churned), and more.

In this scenario challenge, I am tasked to find insights on the following to improve customer retention:

  1. Identify high-value customers. In this analysis, I would define customers as “high-value” based on these factors – retention rate, revenue, sales, and number of referrals.
  2. Identify top reasons for churn. I would explore the customer data based on demographics, customer behavior, and sales to identify which are more likely to contribute to customer churn.

Data cleaning

The following are some of the major steps I did using Power Query in Microsoft Excel to clean and transform the data:

  • Added entries to some identified blank cells that can be filled in with values
  • Assigned categorical names for some numerical data such as age, number of dependents, referrals, tenure, and average download consumption using conditional columns
  • Replaced data in columns containing ‘Yes’ or ‘No’ entries into more descriptive names (e.g., subscribed, not subscribed)
  • Separated the dataset into different tables based on demographics, product, account, and churn data using customer ID as a primary key for Tableau visualization

Data questions

Below are the general questions I would like to find insights on in the analysis of the churn data:

  1. What are the overall customer churn rate, retention rate, and acquisition rate of the telecommunications company?
  2. What are the top reasons for customer churn?
  3. What factors based on demographics, customer behavior, and sales influence customer churn, retention, and acquisition?

To guide my data exploration and dashboard creation, the following questions about the data are specified:

  1. What is the distribution of customers based on demographic factors?
  2. What is the distribution of customers based on product/service subscriptions, marketing offers, and streaming service usage?
  3. What is the distribution of customers based on account activity and sales?

Data findings and insights

Customer churn overview
  1. Most of the customers did not churn by the end of the second quarter of the year, with a retention rate of 67.02%. Customers in this category contributed an average of $3,735.68 in revenue. On average, retained customers have been with the company for 41 months and referred the company to 3 people.
  2. 6.45% of the company’s customers are new this quarter and have an average tenure of 2 months.
  3. Almost a third of the company churned by the end of the quarter, with a churn rate of 26.54%. Compared to the customers who stayed, average revenue, tenure, and number of referrals are generally less. Most of the churned customers are low spenders, are newcomers, and have not referred the company to other people.
  4. The top reason for churn is preference of customers to competitor. According to 45% of churned customers, the competitor offer better devices, better offers, more data, and higher download speeds.
Customer demographics
  1. In terms of age, most of the customers belong to the mid-adulthood age group (36-55 years old).
  2. Even though the customer distribution is almost evenly split in terms of civil status, single customers are more likely to churn (17.038%) than married customers.
  3. 76.90% of the customer base do not have dependents.
  4. The city with the highest churn rate is San Diego (2.627%), while the city with the highest retention rate is Los Angeles (2.797%).
Customer subscriptions
  1. Phone subscribers comprise of 90.32% of the customers. Internet subscribers are 78.33% of the customer base. 68.65% are subscribed to both phone and internet services.
  2. Internet subscription is slightly higher in revenue to phone subscription despite the fact that there are more phone subscribers. Churn rates between both phone and internet subscribers are close to around 24%, with internet subscribers being slightly higher (24.93%).
  3. Churn rates for subscribers availing of other subscriptions such as premium tech support, device protection plan, online security, and online backup are generally less compared to customers who are not subscribed.
Customer offers
  1. Overall acceptance rate of customers to marketing offers is 44.95%. The most accepted offers are Offer B (11.70%) and Offer E (11.43%), while Offer C is the least accepted (5.892%).
  2. Customers who accepted Offer E churned the most with a churn rate of 6.049%.
  3. 10.266% of customers who accepted Offer B stayed with the company, having the highest retention rate among all the marketing offers.
Customer streaming and data usage
  1. The average monthly data download is 20.52 GB, enforcing the fact that most of the customers who are internet subscribers are heavy data users.
  2. Majority of internet subscribers are subscribed to unlimited data (67.37%).
  3. Customers who use their internet to stream movies and TV have lower churn rates.
Customer account activity and sales
  1. The average tenure of customers is 32 months. Overall, customers who have longer tenure like regulars, loyalists, and elite customers churn less compared to newcomers.
  2. Likewise, customers who have longer tenure are more inclined to refer the company to other people. The average number of referrals is 2.
  3. Customers with shorter contracts like month-to-month basis have higher risk of churning, with a churn rate of 23.50%.
  4. Majority of customers are low spenders. These low spenders also churn more compared to customers who pay more for products and other charges.

Final thoughts

Given the tasks I set myself to do in the beginning of this project, here are my final conclusions about the analysis:

  1. High-value customers are those who have been loyal to the company, or those who have longer average tenure. Based from customer behavior and sales data, customers with longer tenure are more likely to spend more on products and services and are more active in referring the company to other people. Customers also churn less the longer their tenure. The company should incentivize customers who have been with them the longest and think of ways to cater their products and services towards them.
  2. The top reason for churn is because of the company’s competitor/s. Areas of weakness identified by customers are the company’s devices, offers, data, and download speeds. As majority of the customers consume high amounts of data, the company should find ways to improve their internet services. Also, the company should look into subscriptions and offers that have affected their churn and retention. For example, something should be improved about Offer E because of the amount of churn it produced. On the other hand, the company should take note of Offer B because of how it performed well in terms of customer retention.
